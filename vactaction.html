Hokkaido Japan

Hokkaido is a small island off the coast of northern Japan. It's known around the world as a winter wonderland with an amazing ski and snowboard season. However, it is also famous for its food, including but not limited to, it's hearty hotpots and spicy curry. Personally I would love to try a local barbeque dish called "Gengis Khan" that uses lamb and vegetables. Lamb is not a popular source of protein in Japan outside of Hokkaido, so you just know it's gotta be good!

Greece

In case it's not already abundantly clear I love most cuisines that use olive oil as a backbone. That being the case it should come as no surprise that Greece is very fight up on my list of places to travel. I'm especially fascinated with finding out good ways to prepare lamb and goat meat. Both are pretty uncommon proteins in the states, so getting the chance to try more dishes with them would be invaluable.

Kerala India

Kerala is a southern state in India and a huge producer of spices. By some estimates India accounts for 75% of the worlds spice production. In Kerala you can see entire courtyards filled with Black Peppercorn, or Cinnamon, drying in the sun, waiting to be shipped. It's the kind of thing that makes me wonder what the air must smell like there. I'll bet it's unbelievable.
